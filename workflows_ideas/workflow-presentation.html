Workflow 2 — Permanent Placement Current State

The core revenue workflow — from requirement to payment. High touch, high risk, high manual effort.

1 Requirement Intake AM
Client sends requirement via email / WhatsApp / call → AM enters in Excel → assigns recruiter(s)
Requirements arrive in different formats. Details often incomplete. Recruiter starts with vague brief.
▼ .
2 Sourcing Recruiter
Search Naukri / LinkedIn → download resumes → add to Excel tracker → store resumes in OneDrive
Same candidate sourced by multiple recruiters. No duplicate detection. Resume versions scattered.
▼ ~40% pass screening
3 Screening Recruiter
Phone calls to assess: skills, current CTC, expected CTC, notice period, communication, interest level
No structured screening form. Assessment quality varies by recruiter. CTC math done in head.
▼ ~60% approved by AM
4 AM Quality Gate AM
AM reviews shortlist → approves or rejects → secondary screen for key/senior roles
Review queue is WhatsApp messages. No SLA tracking. Bottleneck when AM is busy.
▼ ~70% submitted
5 Submission to Client AM
Format resume (remove candidate contact info) → email to client → log submission in Excel
Manual resume formatting. No timestamp tracking. Hard to know "when was this submitted?"
▼ ⏳ Wait 3-10 days for feedback
6 Client Feedback AM chases client
Wait... chase... wait more... ⏳⏳⏳
THE #1 BOTTLENECK. Client goes silent. AM sends follow-up emails. Calls go unanswered. Candidates lose interest while waiting. No escalation path.
▼ ~30-40% shortlisted by client
7 Interview Coordination AM / Recruiter
Schedule interview (back-and-forth on dates/times) → prep candidate → interview happens → collect feedback
Scheduling is 5-10 messages per interview. No calendar integration. Candidate no-shows.
▼ ~30% receive offers
8 Offer & Negotiation AM brokers
Client makes offer → AM brokers CTC negotiation between candidate and client → counter-offer risk from current employer
Counter-offers from current employer are a major risk. No structured risk assessment.
▼ ~75% accept offers
9 Notice Period ⚠️ HIGH RISK Recruiter
30-90 days of waiting. Ad-hoc check-ins. Current employer makes retention offers. Candidate gets cold feet.
15-30% DROP OFF HERE. Revenue earned after months of work vanishes. No structured follow-up cadence. Risk signs missed.
▼ 🔴 15-30% drop off
10 🎉 Joining — Revenue Earned Finance / AM
Candidate joins → manual invoice created → sent to client
▼ ⏳ Wait 90 days for payment
11 💰 Payment (90 days later) Finance
Track payment due date in Excel → follow up with client → chase if overdue → reconcile when received
Cash flow gap: work done in January, payment in April (if on time). Manual tracking. No aging alerts.
Typical conversion: 100 sourced → 40 screened → 24 submitted → 8 shortlisted → 3 offers → 2 join → revenue collected 90 days later

Batch Payment Reconciliation Before & After

The hidden time sink: matching lump-sum client payments to individual invoices

🔴 Today: Manual Reconciliation

Scenario: 10 invoices submitted to Client X

Client sends 3 bank transfers:

Payment 1: ₹4,85,230
  Ref: "INV 001-004"
  ⚠️ Amount doesn't match sum of INV 001-004
    (TDS deducted but amount not stated)

Payment 2: ₹2,10,000
  Ref: "March invoices"
  ⚠️ Which invoices? Nobody knows.

Payment 3: ₹1,50,000
  Ref: "Partial - rest next week"
  ⚠️ Partial against which invoices?

What happens next:

  • Finance person opens Excel, tries to match
  • Calls client AP team for clarification
  • Client says "our system shows those are for INV 005-007"
  • Numbers still don't match — TDS difference
  • More calls, more emails
⏱️ Takes 2-3 days per payment cycle. Error-prone. Invoices marked "paid" that aren't fully paid. Outstanding amounts wrong.

🟢 Future: Smart Reconciliation

Same scenario, platform-assisted:

Step 1: Enter payment: ₹4,85,230

Step 2: Platform suggests:
  📋 INV 001 (₹1,25,000) + INV 002 (₹1,10,000)
    + INV 003 (₹1,50,000) + INV 004 (₹1,20,000)
  Sum: ₹5,05,000
  Expected TDS (2%): ₹10,100
  Expected receipt: ₹4,94,900
  Actual receipt: ₹4,85,230
  ⚠️ Difference: ₹9,670 — investigate

Step 3: One click: Confirm or Flag

Result:

  • Auto-suggests invoice matches based on amounts
  • Auto-calculates expected TDS
  • Highlights discrepancies clearly
  • Partial payments tracked automatically
  • Full audit trail of what was matched when
⏱️ Takes minutes, not days. Errors caught immediately. Outstanding always accurate. Client calls reduced by 80%.

Questions for CEO Review

Please review all workflows above and share your feedback. Here are specific questions we need answered to finalize the platform blueprint.

A. Current Operations (Q1–Q5)

Q1. Client Acquisition Handoff: Is the BD-to-AM handoff described accurately? Who currently receives client requirements — always the AM, or sometimes BD or the CEO directly?
Q2. Requirement Intake: Does the client always communicate the full CTC range upfront, or does StaffSpot sometimes have to estimate? Are requirements always for specific skills, or sometimes vague ("I need a good developer")?
Q3. Quality Gate: Is the AM always the quality gate before client submission, or do senior recruiters sometimes submit directly for requirements they know well?
Q4. Contact Info Removal: Does StaffSpot currently remove candidate contact info from resumes before submission? Is this standard practice for your clients?
Q5. Notice Period Management: How does StaffSpot currently handle the notice period phase? Is there structured follow-up, or is it ad-hoc?

B. Financial (Q6–Q9)

Q6. Payment Terms: Is it always 90 days from joining for permanent placements? Or does it vary by client? Are there any clients who pay faster?
Q7. Timesheets: Who currently collects the timesheets — the contractor submits, or StaffSpot's team collects from the client? Is there a standard format?
Q8. Invoicing: Who generates invoices today — is there a finance person, or does the AM/CEO handle it? What tool is used (Tally? Excel? Manual)?
Q9. Batch Payments: Does the process described (client pays in batches, references specific invoices) match how your clients actually pay? Any clients who pay differently?

C. Strategic (Q10–Q14)

Q10. Compliance: Is compliance handled in-house or outsourced to a CA? Who is responsible for PF/ESI filings today?
Q11. CEO Daily View: Is the information described in the daily dashboard what you would actually want to see? What's missing? What's unnecessary?
Q12. Missing Workflows: Are there any workflows completely missing? For example: how does StaffSpot handle a candidate who is unsuitable after placement? How are recruiter disputes handled? How does the CEO approve/reject expenses?
Q13. Client-Specific Quirks: Are there any client-specific quirks that make the standard workflow different? (e.g., "Client X requires video profiles" or "Client Y only accepts candidates from product companies")
Q14. WhatsApp Centrality: How central is WhatsApp to daily operations? Is candidate communication primarily via WhatsApp? Is client communication also via WhatsApp?

Once these questions are answered, we finalize the workflows and begin building Phase 0.
