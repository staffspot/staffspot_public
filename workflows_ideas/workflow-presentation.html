Workflow 1 — Client Acquisition Current State

From first contact to first requirement — typically 2-8 weeks

1
Lead Generation
👤 BD Team
  • Personal network & CEO contacts
  • LinkedIn prospecting
  • Referrals from placed candidates
  • Industry events (CII/NASSCOM)
  • Monitoring job boards for active hirers
  • Inbound — companies reaching out via website, email, directories
Leads tracked informally or not at all. No follow-up system.
2
Outreach & Discovery
👤 BD / CEO
  • Email / LinkedIn / WhatsApp outreach
  • Discovery meeting (in-person or video)
  • Understand: roles, volume, timeline
  • Assess: payment terms, agency competition
  • Discover: decision-making process, type of staffing needed (permanent/contract/both), budget indicators, payment practices
Meeting notes in personal notebooks. No structured capture.
3
Proposal & Negotiation
👤 BD / CEO
  • Rate card or fee % proposal
  • Margin calculation (manual)
  • Payment terms negotiation
  • Replacement guarantee terms
Rate ranges: Permanent: 8.33%-16.67% of CTC. Contract: 8-15% margin after statutory costs.
Negotiation points: Fee %, payment terms (Net 30-90), guarantee period, exclusivity, non-compete.
Docs: Proposal email, rate card
No standard margin check. Risk of unprofitable deals.
4
MSA Signing
👤 BD / Legal
  • Draft or review MSA
  • Key clauses: scope, fees, payment terms, replacement guarantee, candidate ownership, non-solicitation, NDA/confidentiality, indemnity, termination, compliance responsibility
  • Legal review (CEO at early stage)
  • Sign & file
Docs: Signed MSA
Note: MSA may be signed later for urgent requirements — common but risky.
MSA terms not tracked. No expiry alerts. Clauses forgotten.
5
Handoff to AM
👤 BD → AM
  • Verbal/email context transfer
  • Introduce AM to client contact
  • First requirement received
  • AM assigns recruiter(s)
Operational details collected: How requirements communicated, submission format preference, interview process, billing cycle & contact, holiday calendar (for contract staffing).
Unstructured handoff = context loss. AM asks questions already discussed. Client loses confidence.

1b Requirement Assignment AM

Assignment is informal — based on gut feel, not data. No visibility into recruiter workload.
▼ .
2 Sourcing Recruiter
Search Naukri / LinkedIn → download resumes → add to Excel tracker → store resumes in OneDrive
Same candidate sourced by multiple recruiters. No duplicate detection. Resume versions scattered.
▼
3 Screening Recruiter
Phone calls to assess: skills, current CTC, expected CTC, notice period, communication, interest level
No structured screening form. Assessment quality varies by recruiter. CTC math done in head.

Review queue is WhatsApp messages. No SLA tracking. Bottleneck when AM is busy.

Manual resume formatting. No timestamp tracking. Hard to know "when was this submitted?"

6 Client Feedback AM chases client
Wait... chase... wait more...
THE #1 BOTTLENECK. Client goes silent. AM sends follow-up emails. Calls go unanswered. Candidates lose interest while waiting. No escalation path.
▼
7 Interview Coordination AM / Recruiter
Schedule interview (back-and-forth on dates/times) → prep candidate → interview happens
Scheduling is 5-10 messages per interview. No calendar integration. Candidate no-shows.
▼ .
7b Feedback Collection AM / Recruiter
Collect structured feedback from BOTH sides — candidate's assessment of the interview AND client's rating/decision. This is a separate critical step.
Feedback is often one-sided (only client) or delayed. Candidate experience is not captured. Decisions drag.
▼

Client makes offer → AM brokers CTC negotiation between candidate and client → counter-offer risk from current employer
Counter-offers from current employer are a major risk. No structured risk assessment.

9 Notice Period Recruiter
Offer acceptance → written confirmation → offer letter from client → candidate resigns → serves notice (30-90 days).
Ad-hoc check-ins. Current employer makes retention offers. Candidate gets cold feet.
15-30% DROP OFF HERE. Revenue earned after months of work vanishes. No structured follow-up cadence. Risk signs missed.

Invoice: Fee % × CTC + GST. CGST+SGST (same state) or IGST (different state). Needs PO number for some clients.

Contract Staffing: Sourcing to Deployment

Phase A — From requirement intake to contractor starting at client site (Steps 1-5)

1
Requirement Intake
👤 AM / BD
  • Bill rate agreed with client
  • SOW drafted
  • Margin calculated
Bill rate - salary - PF 13% - ESI 3.25% - PT - bonus 8.33% - overhead = margin. Need ≥8-10% margin to be viable.
2
Sourcing & Screening
👤 Recruiter
  • Same as permanent BUT: pay rate negotiation is critical
  • Every ₹1,000 error in pay rate directly hits margin
  • Candidate must agree to be on StaffSpot's payroll, not client's
3
Client Selection & Offer
👤 AM
  • Faster than permanent (1-2 interview rounds)
  • Verbal offer with CTC, contract duration, start date
4
Contractor Onboarding
👤 HR / AM
  • Appointment letter from StaffSpot (not client)
  • PF enrollment (UAN via EPFO portal, needs Aadhaar)
  • ESI enrollment (if salary ≤ ₹21,000/month)
  • Professional Tax registration (state-wise)
  • Bank details, NDA
Document checklist: Aadhaar, PAN, bank proof, photo, certificates.
5
Deployment
👤 AM
  • Contractor starts at client site
  • Day-1 confirmation
  • Assignment record created: contractor → client → role → bill rate → pay rate → margin → dates → reporting manager

Margin Math Example

Client bill rate (monthly):          ₹80,000
- Contractor gross salary:          ₹50,000
- PF employer (13%):                ₹6,500
- ESI employer (3.25%):              ₹1,625
- Professional Tax:                 ₹200
- Bonus provision (8.33%):           ₹4,165
- Admin/insurance overhead:          ₹1,500
= StaffSpot gross margin:           ₹16,010 (20%)
GST charged on top to client — not part of margin

Contract Staffing: Lifecycle Events

Phase C — Renewal, exit, and redeployment (Steps 15-17)

1. Contract Renewal / Extension

  • System alerts AM 60 days before contract end
  • Check with client: extend? Rate change?
  • Check with contractor: continue? Salary increase?
  • Rate renegotiation: contractor wants raise, client resists bill rate increase → margin compression risk
  • Contract-to-permanent conversion: common after 12+ months, involves conversion fee per SOW
  • New SOW or amendment issued

2. Contract End / Exit

  • Last working day confirmed
  • Client-side: return assets, revoke access
  • Full & Final (F&F): final salary + leave encashment - recoveries
  • Exit documents: relieving letter, experience certificate, final payslip, Form 16
  • PF: guide contractor through withdrawal (Form 19) or transfer (Form 13) on EPFO portal
  • ESI: medical benefits continue for a period after exit
  • Exit conversation: feedback, willingness to be redeployed

3. Redeployment

  • Move contractor to different client/role
  • PF/ESI continuity (same employer — StaffSpot)
  • New SOW, possibly new rates
  • Bench period tracking: if gap between assignments — contractor on payroll but unbilled = pure cost
Bench days are direct cost to StaffSpot. Must be tracked and minimized.

Batch Payment Reconciliation Before & After

The hidden time sink: matching lump-sum client payments to individual invoices

🔴 Today: Manual Reconciliation

Scenario: 10 invoices submitted to Client X

Client sends 3 bank transfers:

Payment 1: ₹4,85,230
  Ref: "INV 001-004"
  ⚠️ Amount doesn't match sum of INV 001-004
    (TDS deducted but amount not stated)

Payment 2: ₹2,10,000
  Ref: "March invoices"
  ⚠️ Which invoices? Nobody knows.

Payment 3: ₹1,50,000
  Ref: "Partial - rest next week"
  ⚠️ Partial against which invoices?

What happens next:

  • Finance person opens Excel, tries to match
  • Calls client AP team for clarification
  • Client says "our system shows those are for INV 005-007"
  • Numbers still don't match — TDS difference
  • More calls, more emails
⏱️ Takes 2-3 days per payment cycle. Error-prone. Invoices marked "paid" that aren't fully paid. Outstanding amounts wrong.

🟢 Future: Smart Reconciliation

Same scenario, platform-assisted:

Step 1: Enter payment: ₹4,85,230

Step 2: Platform suggests:
  📋 INV 001 (₹1,25,000) + INV 002 (₹1,10,000)
    + INV 003 (₹1,50,000) + INV 004 (₹1,20,000)
  Sum: ₹5,05,000
  Expected TDS (2%): ₹10,100
  Expected receipt: ₹4,94,900
  Actual receipt: ₹4,85,230
  ⚠️ Difference: ₹9,670 — investigate

Step 3: One click: Confirm or Flag

Result:

  • Auto-suggests invoice matches based on amounts
  • Auto-calculates expected TDS
  • Highlights discrepancies clearly
  • Partial payments tracked automatically
  • Full audit trail of what was matched when
  • After reconciliation: triggers contractor salary payment for matched invoices
  • TDS certificate tracking: Form 16A tracked quarterly
  • Future: Bank CSV import for auto-matching (not just manual entry)
⏱️ Takes minutes, not days. Errors caught immediately. Outstanding always accurate.

Statutory Compliance Calendar

Monthly and periodic filing deadlines — missing these means penalties

Deadline Filing Penalty if Late
7th of month TDS deposit (salary TDS) 1.5% per month interest
11th of month GSTR-1 (outward invoices) ₹50/day
15th of month PF ECR (monthly PF contribution) 12% p.a. interest + up to 100% damages
15th of month ESI contribution 12% p.a. interest
15th-30th (varies) Professional Tax (state-wise) State-specific
20th of month GSTR-3B (summary + tax payment) 18% p.a. interest
Quarterly (Jul/Oct/Jan/May) TDS Return (Form 24Q) ₹200/day penalty
15th June annually Form 16 to contractors ₹100/day per certificate
Platform automation: The platform will auto-calculate all deadlines, send 7-day and 3-day advance alerts, track filing status, and store all receipts.

Questions for CEO Review

Please review all workflows above and share your feedback. Here are specific questions we need answered to finalize the platform blueprint.

A. Current Operations (Q1–Q5)

Q1. Client Acquisition Handoff: Is the BD-to-AM handoff described accurately? Who currently receives client requirements — always the AM, or sometimes BD or the CEO directly?
Q2. Requirement Intake: Does the client always communicate the full CTC range upfront, or does StaffSpot sometimes have to estimate? Are requirements always for specific skills, or sometimes vague ("I need a good developer")?
Q3. Quality Gate: Is the AM always the quality gate before client submission, or do senior recruiters sometimes submit directly for requirements they know well?
Q4. Contact Info Removal: Does StaffSpot currently remove candidate contact info from resumes before submission? Is this standard practice for your clients?
Q5. Notice Period Management: How does StaffSpot currently handle the notice period phase? Is there structured follow-up, or is it ad-hoc?

B. Financial (Q6–Q9)

Q6. Payment Terms: Is it always 90 days from joining for permanent placements? Or does it vary by client? Are there any clients who pay faster?
Q7. Timesheets: Who currently collects the timesheets — the contractor submits, or StaffSpot's team collects from the client? Is there a standard format?
Q8. Invoicing: Who generates invoices today — is there a finance person, or does the AM/CEO handle it? What tool is used (Tally? Excel? Manual)?
Q9. Batch Payments: Does the process described (client pays in batches, references specific invoices) match how your clients actually pay? Any clients who pay differently?

C. Strategic (Q10–Q14)

Q10. Compliance: Is compliance handled in-house or outsourced to a CA? Who is responsible for PF/ESI filings today?
Q11. CEO Daily View: Is the information described in the daily dashboard what you would actually want to see? What's missing? What's unnecessary?
Q12. Missing Workflows: Are there any workflows completely missing? For example: how does StaffSpot handle a candidate who is unsuitable after placement? How are recruiter disputes handled? How does the CEO approve/reject expenses?
Q13. Client-Specific Quirks: Are there any client-specific quirks that make the standard workflow different? (e.g., "Client X requires video profiles" or "Client Y only accepts candidates from product companies")
Q14. WhatsApp Centrality: How central is WhatsApp to daily operations? Is candidate communication primarily via WhatsApp? Is client communication also via WhatsApp?

Once these questions are answered, we finalize the workflows and begin building Phase 0.
